What is Algol 68 Genie?

Algol 68 Genie (Algol68G) is an Algol 68 interpreter. It can be used for executing Algol 68 programs or scripts. Algol 68 is a rather lean orthogonal general-purpose language that is a beautiful means for denoting algorithms. Algol 68 was designed as a general-purpose programming language by IFIP Working Group 2.1 (Algorithmic Languages and Calculi) that has continuing responsibility for Algol 60 and Algol 68.

Since Algol 68 Genie became available on the Internet, it has become clear that there still are many people who are interested in an Algol 68 implementation, the majority of them being mathematicians or computer scientists. The language is nowadays still referred to in teaching material, discussions and publications. Because of the exceptional rigour and precision of its definition, one could argue that a student reading computer science should, at an appropriate moment, study Algol 68, irrespective of whether the language did or did not spread widely at the time it was presented.

For programmers, the world has of course moved on, but the reactions on Algol 68 Genie suggest that many people who have seriously programmed in Algol 68 in the past, only moved to other programming languages because the Algol 68 implementations they were using were phased out.

The observations mentioned here are believed to be sufficient ground to preserve Algol 68, both by making available its defining document as well as an implementation as to enable those interested in the language to both study it, and use it.

Obtaining Algol 68 Genie

The current version of Algol 68 Genie is Mark 12, released in May 2008. You can download it from this page. Algol 68 Genie (Algol68G) is free software, distributed under GNU General Public License. Consult the GNU General Public License for details. The distribution of Algol 68 Genie Mark 12 contains revision 1.1 of the HTML translation of the Algol 68 Revised Report.

Download Algol68G Mark 12, a gzipped tar archive containing C source code, a Win32 executable, documentation and revision 1.1 of the HTML translation of the Algol 68 Revised Report.

What libraries can Algol 68 Genie link to?

  • GNU Plotutils for drawing from Algol 68.
  • GNU Scientific Library to extend the mathematical capabilities of the standard environ.
  • PostgreSQL for writing PostgreSQL database client applications.
